The seed grows into a presence...

Whojr, Inc.’s strength is in listening, understanding your idea, and working with you to make it into a tangible website that builds presence.

Users

Whomever, visits your website creates your Presence from the Inception of your idea, as a seed that grows, if they enjoy their experience on your site.

The user experience, simply defined, is what got you from my main page to this page, curiosity. The central theme for exploring is curiosity, although curiosity isn’t enough, the user also needs a purpose and that purpose has to align with your design.

Creating the user experience is about feeding their curiosity through content and purpose. The visuals of the site, the controls, how they get from the main page to the checkout box fortifies that experience.

Analytics

The systematic computational analysis of data or statistics really doesn’t sound as exciting as it can be, until it tells the story about the visitors to your site (The User Experience).

The measurement of how successful your design is in triggering the curiosity factor, how they interact with your site, is through website analytics. In essence analytics connects the user experience to the goals of the site and tells the story of when the two met.

A website development does not stop at the creation of a few web pages, it is only the Inception, what happens or doesn’t happen after that determines your Presence. How your users view your website is a measurable experience.

customers

On the internet, you can pay to attract users to your site or you can let your customers find you through word of mouth. The former is called Cost per Click as you pay for internet ads and the latter is FREE through user curiosity or word of mouth.

Your website must have a single focused goal to inform users, generate leads, or generate sales. The success of your website can be measured to determine if you’re meeting or exceeding the goal you set (Analytics).

Finding new customers is the key for the survival of most businesses. How you generate leads, provide services, or entertain are all factors that must be considered in the design of your site. After all, it’s the user experience that is going to define your presence, keep them on the site longer and share with their friends.

Inception

Delivering on a custom website can be challenging, but it's always based on your needs. It could be as simple as using, HTML/CSS with a bit of PHP/Javascript and a database, very similar to how this site was created.

However, your needs more than likely differ from mine, and a content management system like WordPress might suit your needs better with a responsive theme to reach the greatest audience.

The key in selecting any technology is to focus on the business goal and how the business operates. Then determine if a suitable user experience is supported by the technology that supports the devices used by your target audience.
